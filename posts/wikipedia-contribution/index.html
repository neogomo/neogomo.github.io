Diagram for Wikipedia Article

Oct. 15, 2024

My little contribution to Wikipedia are these two visualizations to accompany an article. Here is how I created them – or to be more precise – where I found the original one, and used it to create a visual copy of it, but based on a modified pixel structure underneath it.

You can find these two visualizations on Wikipedia. They are part of the article about Pixel aspect ratios (PAR) in digital imaging. Another contributor over there took the time to re-create them in SVG format, which is very commendable.

My idea for them was to show that even if you display images on devices that make use of non-square pixels (either physically as a diplay pixel, or logically at any rendering stage), they finally result in visually the same images, that is given a high enough resolution, and or large enough distance to the observer.

To better illustrate this on a magnified portion of a small image area, I thought that bands of varying color, preferably at an angle other than 90°, could help to prove that point. And luckily enough, I even had a photo in mind, that I had taken some while ago.

You may be curious, what object does show such delicate bands of color? – Glad you asked, here you go:

Munich in 2004, Canon EOS 10D
